Adding OG Images to Your Website

Once you have your image, you need to add the right <meta> tags to your HTML <head>. Here's a complete setup that covers Open Graph (Facebook, LinkedIn, Slack, Discord) and Twitter Cards: 

<!-- Primary Open Graph tags -->
<meta property="og:title" content="Your Page Title">
<meta property="og:description" content="A compelling description.">
<meta property="og:image" content="https://yoursite.com/og-image.png">
<meta property="og:image:width" content="1200">
<meta property="og:image:height" content="630">
<meta property="og:type" content="website">

<!-- Twitter Card tags -->
<meta name="twitter:card" content="summary_large_image">
<meta name="twitter:title" content="Your Page Title">
<meta name="twitter:description" content="A compelling description.">
<meta name="twitter:image" content="https://yoursite.com/og-image.png">

After adding these tags, validate your OG image using these free tools: opengraph.xyz (shows previews for all platforms) or Twitter Card Validator.

OG Image Best Practices

  • Dimensions: 1200x630 pixels — this is the universal standard. Facebook recommends 1.91:1 ratio, and 1200x630 hits it perfectly.
  • Keep text in the center — platforms crop OG images differently on mobile vs desktop. Leave 60px safe margins on all sides.
  • Use your brand domain — putting your domain on the image builds recognition. When people scroll past, they know it's from you.
  • Contrast matters — dark backgrounds with bright text (or light backgrounds with dark text) render best at the small sizes social platforms display them.
  • Test on real platforms — Facebook, Twitter, LinkedIn, Slack, and Discord all render OG images slightly differently. Test at least the top 2 platforms where your audience lives.
  • Use PNG, not JPEG — PNG handles text and gradients better. Keep file size under 300KB for fast loading.
